One of the greatest miracles is the Qur'an itself. Revealed to Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him), it is considered a timeless miracle for its unmatched linguistic beauty, deep guidance, and preservation over centuries. It continues to inspire millions and offers answers to both spiritual and worldly matters. Throughout history, Allah granted miracles to His prophets as signs for their people. For example, Prophet Musa (peace be upon him) parted the sea by Allah’s command, and Prophet Isa (peace be upon him) healed the sick and gave life to the dead with Allah’s permission. These miracles were not acts of the prophets themselves, but manifestations of Allah’s will. Even today, miracles can be seen in everyday life—healing from illness, unexpected رزق (provision), or moments when things fall into place in ways beyond human planning. These are subtle reminders that Allah is always near, always listening, and always قادر (all-powerful). Believers are encouraged to recognize these signs, strengthen their faith, and trust in Allahs plan. True miracles are not only in extraordinary events but also in the guidance, peace, and patience Allah grants to the hearts of those who believe.
